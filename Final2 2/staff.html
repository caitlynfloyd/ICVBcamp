Staff







Johan Dulfer


Ithaca College Head Coach

Johan Dulfer assumed the role of Head Coach at Ithaca College in March of 2016 after a decade long run of unparalleled success at Clarkson University. In only his second year at the helm, coach Dulfer saw his Bombers enjoy an outstanding 2017 with a 25-9 record, their first-ever Liberty League championship, and a magical run through the NCAA tournament that saw them advancing all the way to the Final Four. It was the deepest post season run in the 50-year program history.

Coach Dulfer has been lucky to have coached some tremendously successful teams. In his 10 years at Clarkson, coach Dulfer orchestrated the turnaround of the Golden Knight program into a National Powerhouse. Clarkson qualified for 7 straight NCAA tournament appearances on his watch, and reached the Elite Eight 4 times in that time span (2012-2015).

Coach Dulfer believes that to create extraordinary results, you have to be willing to think -and train- outside the box. His teams are known for their high volleyball IQ, and their passionate approach to the game. Coach Dulfer was voted the Liberty League coach of the year 4 times and the New York regional coach of the year 3 times as his teams put together the most dominant resume of any team in the country in the past 4 seasons.

Coach Dulfer has coached over 15 All Americans as well as 2 Academic All Americans. Some of them started their careers by coming to the same summer camp you are thinking about signing up for!







Alesia Vaccari


Brandeis University Head Coach



Alesia Vaccari became the head coach at Brandeis University in June of 2014. Prior to coming to Brandeis, Alesia was the head coach at Simmons College where she posted a 50-49 record with the Sharks. Her teams reached the GNAC semifinals each of her three years with the program, earning her GNAC Coach of the Year honors in her first year, and the 2012 AVCA "Thirty under Thirty", which recognizes the 30 top up and coming coaches in the country.

Coach Vaccari worked with coach Dulfer at Clarkson University, bringing the University its first-ever Liberty League championship in 2009 and advancing to the NCAA tournament. Coach Vaccari and Coach Dulfer were the 2010 Liberty League coaching staff of the year.

Alesia is a former All-American setter at Springfield College, where her name is all over the record books. The same competitive fire that made coach Vaccari an All-American, is pushing Brandeis closer and closer to the top of the powerful UAA conference. Get ready to learn a lot from one of the best young coaches in the game.






Mackenzie Pelton


IVA Director of Operations







Coach Pelton joins the IVA staff as the Director of Volleyball Operations (DOVO) for the Ithaca Volleyball Academy, but is no stranger to Coach Dulfer or the 360 Training Concepts program.

A former player in the Clarkson Golden Knight program, Pelton both played and worked inside the Clarkson program from 2012-2015. In addition to her work within the Clarkson Volleyball program, Coach Pelton worked closely with athletes for four years at the Nike Volleyball Camps.

Kaley Vogel


Assistant Coach

Kaley Vogel (Viola) returns for her third Ithaca Volleyball Academy. As the head assistant coach on coach Dulfer's staff, she was part of the magical 2017 season that saw her Bombers advance all the way to the Final Four. Coach Viola, known for her personal style and tactical knowledge of the game, has an extensive background in Division I. Her name is all over the record books at Winthrop University in South Carolina. As an assistant, she had successful stops at her alma mater, as well as Davidson, before joining Ithaca.

In her first season as an assistant on head coach Johan Dulfer's staff, Vogel helped the Bombers to a 27-6 record, which included an Empire 8 Championship and trip to the Second Round of the NCAA Division III Tournament.

Vogel came to Ithaca after a two-year stint at Division I Davidson College as an assistant and recruiting coordinator. Prior to Davidson, Vogel served as an assistant coach at her alma mater, Winthrop University, in 2013, and helped guide the Eagles to a 17-13 overall record. From 2011 to 2013, she was a graduate assistant strength and conditioning coach at the University.

Along with her collegiate coaching positions, Vogel has coached club teams and helped run various camps and clinics for the past eight years. In the summer of 2014, she volunteered with the U.S. National team in Anaheim, Calif.

As a student-athlete, Vogel was a four-year member of the Winthrop volleyball team from 2007-2010. She finished her career with 3,323 total attacks, which ranks her seventh all-time, 1,056 kills, 996 digs and 163 total blocks. She earned Big South second team all-conference honors in 2009 and 2010.







Kelsey Klopfer


Assistant Coach

Coach Klopfer assisted the program to a 21-8 record in her debut on South Hill in 2018, helping the program capture a second consecutive NCAA Regional Championship and berth to the NCAA Quarterfinals. Four student-athletes earned All-Liberty League praise under her tutelage, including sophomore right-side hitter Reagan Stone capturing Liberty League Performer of the Year honors. Three Bombers went on to earn AVCA All-Region praise in Stone, junior setter Caitlyn Floyd, and senior middle hitter Katie Evans. Stone and Floyd went on to earn Second- and Third-Team All-America honors, the first time the program has posted multiple All-Americans on one of the three teams since 1995.

Klopfer joined the program after spending two seasons as the head coach of Onondaga Community College’s women’s volleyball program for the past two seasons. Taking over the program in 2016 after serving as an assistant coach during the 2015 campaign, Klopfer led the Lazers to a 48-19 record during her two seasons in charge. She also helped guide six players to All-Conference honors, four to All-Region accolades, while she had one MSAC Player of the Year and one MSAC Defensive Player of the Year honoree during her time with the program. She will be Ithaca’s new assistant coach, taking the role of former assistant Kaley Vogel, who moved on following two excellent seasons with the program in 2016 and 2017.






Annie McShea


Stevens Institute Head Coach





Coach McShea was hired as the head coach at Stevens Institute of Technology in 2017 and made a splash in her very first season as the Ducks' head coach. Her team captured the Empire 8 regular season and tournament championships and advanced to the Round of 32 of the NCAA tournament where they played the toughest match of the New York regional tournament against Ithaca College. Coach McShea was the assistant coach at Midwest powerhouse Millikin, and before that she helped guide Clarkson to 2 Elite Eight appearances working alongside coach Dulfer. Rivals during the fall season, coach Dulfer and coach McShea are happy to be reunited at the Ithaca Volleyball Academy for a week during the summer.
